The History of the Olympics

Objective: Talk about history and facts using the Passive Voice.

1. Vocabulary: Events & Discoveries

Learn these verbs to describe how events are organized or how things are created.

🏟️ Hold an event Realizar/Celebrar un evento V3: Held
🌍 Host a competition Ser anfitrión/Alojar V3: Hosted
🥇 Award a medal Otorgar una medalla V3: Awarded
💡 Invent a sport Inventar un deporte V3: Invented

2. Grammar: The Spotlight Shift (Passive Voice)

In standard sentences (Active Voice), the spotlight is on WHO did the action. But in history and news, we often don't know who did it, or it's obvious. So, we shift the spotlight to WHAT happened using the Passive Voice.

PAST PASSIVE (History)
Was / Were + Past Participle (V3)

"The first Olympic Games were held in Greece."

(We care about the games, not the specific people who organized them).

PRESENT PASSIVE (Facts)
Am / Is / Are + Past Participle (V3)

"Today, gold medals are awarded to the winners."

(We care about the medals, not the Olympic committee giving them).

🚨 THE FORGOTTEN "TO BE"

The most common mistake at the B1 level is forgetting the verb "To Be" when making a passive sentence. The verb To Be is the engine of the passive voice!

❌ The telephone invented in 1876. (This means the telephone grew arms and invented something!)
✅ The telephone WAS invented in 1876.
