2. Grammar: Projecting into the Future

Imagine you travel to December of next year. You look back at everything you have done. This is exactly what the Future Perfect does!

The magic word for these tenses is "BY" (Para / Antes de), which creates a deadline in the future.

TenseFormulaUse
Future Perfect Simple will have + V3 To say an action will be completed before a certain time in the future.
(By Friday, I will have finished the app).
Future Perfect Continuous will have been + V-ing To emphasize the duration of an action up to a certain point in the future.
(By next year, we will have been living here for 3 years).
🚨 The "Until" vs "By" Trap

In Spanish, we sometimes mix "hasta" y "para" when talking about deadlines. In B2 English, they are strictly different.

UNTIL = An action continues and then stops (I will sleep until 10 AM).
BY = A deadline for completion (I will have finished the report by 10 AM).

❌ I will have graded the exams until tomorrow.
✅ I will have graded the exams BY tomorrow.
