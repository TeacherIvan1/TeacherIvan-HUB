2. Key Grammar: Wh- Questions (To Be)

We use Wh- words (What, Where, Who) + Verb To Be (am, is, are) to ask for personal information.

Wh- WordVerb To BeSubjectExample Answer
Whatisyour name?My name is Ivan.
Whatisyour email address?It is ivan@fielding.com
Whatisyour phone number?It is 7-2-5-4-3-8-9
Whereareyou from?I am from Sucre, Bolivia.
🗣️ SPEAKING TIP: Reading Emails & Numbers

In English, we read phone numbers one by one (7-2-5...). For the number zero, we usually say "Oh".

For email addresses, remember:
@ is pronounced "at".
. is pronounced "dot".

Let's see it in context: The New Athlete

Teacher Ivan: Hello! Welcome to the CrossFit gym. What is your name?

Carlos: Hello! My name is Carlos.

Teacher Ivan: Nice to meet you, Carlos. Where are you from?

Carlos: I am from La Paz, but I live in Sucre now.

Teacher Ivan: Great. I need your contact info. What is your phone number?

Carlos: It is 6-5-3-2-8-9-oh.

Teacher Ivan: And what is your email address for the receipts?

Carlos: It is carlos@gmail.com (carlos at gmail dot com).
