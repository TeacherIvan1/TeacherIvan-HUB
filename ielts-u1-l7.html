Map Comparisons

Objective: Describe urban and architectural transformations using the Passive Voice and spatial prepositions.

1. Academic Vocabulary: Verbs of Transformation

When a map changes, buildings don't just "go away" or "appear". You must use precise architectural verbs in the Passive Voice.

🏗️ Constructed Construido (Edificios nuevos) Synonyms: Built / Erected
💥 Demolished Demolido (Destruido) Synonyms: Knocked down / Torn down
🔄 Converted into Convertido en (Cambio de uso) Synonym: Transformed into
🌲 Cleared Despejado (Árboles/Bosques) Synonym: Cut down

2. Spatial Language & Map Grammar

To score highly on maps, you must master two things: 1) Compass Directions (North, South, East, West) and 2) The "A became B" structure.

Structure: Replaced By
[Old Thing] + was demolished and replaced by + [New Thing]

Use this when an old building is destroyed to put a new one in its exact place.

"The cafeteria was demolished and replaced by a gym."
Structure: To make way for
[Old Thing] + was knocked down to make way for + [New Thing]

Use this to explain the *reason* why something was destroyed.

"The trees were cleared to make way for a parking lot."
🚨 THE PREPOSITION TRAP (Location)

Students constantly lose points by using the wrong prepositions for compass directions. Memorize these rules:

  • IN the north OF the town: It is inside the town's borders.
  • TO the north OF the town: It is outside, pointing north from the town.
  • ON the north side: Bordering the northern edge.
