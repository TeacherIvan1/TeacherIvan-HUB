Healing a Sports Injury

Objective: Express duration using the Present Perfect with 'For' and 'Since'.

1. Vocabulary: Ouch! Sports Injuries

Learn how to describe common accidents during physical training.

🤕 Sprain an ankle Esguinzarse un tobillo V3: Sprained
💥 Pull a muscle Desgarrar un músculo V3: Pulled
🩹 Wear a bandage Llevar un vendaje V3: Worn
🧊 Put ice on it Ponerle hielo V3: Put (Irregular)

2. Grammar: How long? (For vs Since)

When you have an injury, people always ask: "How long have you had it?" (¿Cuánto tiempo lo has tenido?). To answer, we use the Present Perfect with two special words.

FOR
Use it for a Duration (Amount of time).
(Formula: Number + Time word)
  • ... for two days
  • ... for three weeks
  • ... for a long time
  • ... for five months
SINCE
Use it for a Starting Point.
(Formula: A point on the calendar/clock)
  • ... since Monday
  • ... since October
  • ... since 2024
  • ... since I was a child
🚨 THE "DESDE HACE" TRAP

In Spanish, we say "Desde hace 5 días". Many B1 students try to translate this literally and say "Since 5 days" or "Since 5 days ago". This is 100% WRONG in English!

✅ If there is a NUMBER of days/weeks/years, ALWAYS use FOR.
➔ "I have had this pain FOR 5 days."

Reading Context: An Injury at the Gym

Sarah: Teacher Ivan, why are you walking so slowly today? Are you okay?

Teacher Ivan: Not really. I have pulled a muscle in my leg. I was doing heavy squats at the CrossFit gym.

Sarah: Oh no! How long have you had this pain?

Teacher Ivan: I have had it since Saturday. So, I haven't trained for three days.

Sarah: Have you put ice on it?

Teacher Ivan: Yes, my girlfriend is helping me. I have worn a compression bandage since this morning, but it still hurts. At least Perlita the cat is happy because I am resting on the sofa with her!
