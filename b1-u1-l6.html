Have you ever been to...?

Objective: Distinguish between "Been" and "Gone" when talking about travel experiences.

1. Vocabulary: Travel Prep

Before we talk about your experiences, let's learn some travel vocabulary.

🧳 Pack a suitcase Hacer la maleta V3: Packed
✈️ Book a flight Reservar un vuelo V3: Booked
🌍 Go abroad Ir al extranjero V3: Gone abroad
📸 Go sightseeing Hacer turismo V3: Gone sightseeing

2. Grammar: Been vs. Gone

The verb "Go" is tricky in the Present Perfect. It has TWO past participles: Been and Gone. They mean completely different things. Think of them as plane tickets!

BEEN
✈️ ➔ 🏠
The Return Ticket. You went to a place, and you came back. You are sharing an experience.
"I have been to Argentina."
(I am in Sucre right now)
GONE
✈️ ➔ 🏖️
The One-Way Ticket. You went to a place, and you are STILL THERE. You haven't returned.
"She has gone to the gym."
(She is not at home right now)
🚨 THE PREPOSITION TRAP

When we ask about travel experiences, Spanish speakers often say "Have you ever been IN Europe?". This is a common mistake!

❌ I have been IN Brazil.
✅ I have been TO Brazil. (Always use "TO" for travel experiences).

Reading Context: Where is everyone?

Sarah: Teacher Ivan, have you ever been to Europe?

Teacher Ivan: No, I haven't. But I have been to Argentina. My girlfriend and I traveled there for a CrossFit tournament a few years ago.

Sarah: That’s cool! Oh, I wanted to ask her something. Is your girlfriend here?

Teacher Ivan: No, she has gone to the supermarket. She left ten minutes ago to buy some avocados for dinner.

Sarah: And Perlita?

Teacher Ivan: Perlita has gone to the garden. She is probably sleeping in the sun right now!
