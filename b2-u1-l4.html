2. Grammar: The "Used To" Triplets

One of the biggest challenges in B2 is mastering the word "Used". It changes its meaning completely depending on the verbs around it. Let's look at the three forms:

1. The Past Habit
Used to + BASE VERB
A habit or state in the past that is NO LONGER TRUE today. ("Solía...")
"I used to sleep until 9:00 AM."
(But not anymore).
2. The Adaptation Process
Get used to + NOUN / ING
The process of something becoming normal. It is still a little difficult. ("Acostumbrándose a...")
"I am getting used to waking up at 5:30 AM."
(It is getting easier).
3. The Present Normality
Be used to + NOUN / ING
The adaptation is complete. The action is 100% normal and easy for you now. ("Estar acostumbrado a...")
"I am used to the cold mornings in Sucre."
(It doesn't bother me).
🚨 The B2 "ING" Trap!

You were taught that after the preposition "TO", you must use the Base Verb (e.g., I want to go). But with "Be used to" and "Get used to", the word "to" acts as a connector to a noun/gerund.

❌ I am used to wake up early.
✅ I am used to WAKING up early. (Add -ING!)

Reading Context: Teacher Ivan's Morning Routine

"When I was younger, I used to hit snooze on my alarm five times. I used to hate mornings! But now, my life is different. Because I teach at Colegio Fielding and train CrossFit, I have to wake up at 5:30 AM.

At first, it was a terrible struggle. I couldn't stay awake in the afternoons. I had to get used to drinking a lot of coffee. It took me months to get used to the cold weather in Sucre at that hour.

However, today, I am used to waking up in the dark. It feels completely normal. In fact, I love it! The only one who hasn't adapted is Perlita. She used to wake me up for food, but now she sleeps until 10:00 AM. She will never get used to the 5:30 AM Club!"
