Complex Tables

Objective: Navigate data grids by identifying extremes and using superlative structures.

1. Academic Vocabulary: Superlatives & Proximity

When looking at a table, you must identify the highest, the lowest, and what comes second.

🔝 The highest figure La cifra más alta Synonym: The peak / The maximum
🥈 Closely followed by Seguido de cerca por... Use: To introduce the 2nd place
🔻 Ranked last Ocupó el último lugar Synonym: The least popular
🤏 A negligible amount Una cantidad insignificante Synonym: An insignificant figure

2. The "Extremes" Strategy

A table might have 20 or 30 different numbers. If you describe every single cell, you will run out of time and lose points for bad organization. You must use the "Extremes Strategy".

1. Find the Maximums
Subject + recorded + the highest proportion of...

Scan the table for the largest numbers and group them together.

"Group A recorded the highest number of hours."
2. Find the Minimums
Subject + accounted for + a negligible amount

Scan the table for the zeros or the lowest numbers.

"Group C allocated a negligible amount of time to sports."
🚨 THE SHOPPING LIST TRAP

Do NOT write: "A1 is 5. A2 is 7. B1 is 10. B2 is 12." The examiner calls this a "shopping list". It shows zero analytical skill. You must compare and synthesize!

✅ "Hours spent studying rose consistently as the English level increased, peaking at 12 for the B2 group."
