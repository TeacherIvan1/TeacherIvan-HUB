2. Grammar: Advanced Quantifiers

In B1, you learned "much" and "many". Now in B2, we need to express psychology and exact amounts. The most important rule is the Power of "A". Adding the letter "A" changes the meaning from negative to positive!

A few / A little
POSITIVE (Some / Enough)

You have a small amount, but it is enough. You are happy.

"I have a few almonds for a snack." (I'm okay).
"I have a little energy left." (I can do one more rep).
Few / Little
NEGATIVE (Not enough)

You have almost zero. It is not enough. You are sad/worried.

"I have few almonds." (I am still starving!).
"I have little energy left." (I need to stop now).

More B2 Upgrades:

  • Instead of "A lot of", use ➔ Plenty of (More than enough).
  • Instead of "Almost zero", use ➔ Hardly any (Casi nada).
🚨 The Countable Trap

Never mix them up! Use Few / A Few for things you can count with numbers (Calories, meals, almonds, reps). Use Little / A Little for uncountable concepts (Protein, water, time, energy, sugar).

Reading Context: The CrossFit Diet

"When my girlfriend and I prepare for a CrossFit competition, tracking our diet is essential. We have to measure our portions carefully to make sure we get plenty of lean protein.

Living in Sucre, the altitude makes you dehydrated quickly, so we drink a great deal of water. I try to cut down on junk food, so I eat hardly any sugar during the week. Sometimes, I eat a few strawberries before training just to get a little extra energy.

Unfortunately, Perlita the cat doesn't understand macros. She burns very few calories because she just sleeps all day. She shows little interest in my healthy salads; she only wants her tuna!"
