Past Simple vs Present Perfect

Objective: Combine both tenses correctly to share general news and specific details.

1. Vocabulary: Life Milestones

Learn these verbs to talk about big projects and important moments in your life.

📱 Develop an app Desarrollar una app Past: Developed / V3: Developed

2. Grammar: The "Zoom In" Technique

Native speakers do not use just one tense. They mix them! We use the Present Perfect to announce the "Big News" (the general experience), and then we Zoom In using the Past Simple to give the specific details (when, where, who).

🌍
Step 1: The Big Picture (Present Perfect)

Introduce the general life experience. No specific time.

"I have traveled to many cities in Bolivia."
🔍
Step 2: Zoom In for Details (Past Simple)

Give the specific details. Use time markers like yesterday, last year, in 2024.

"For example, I went to La Paz last year for a CrossFit competition."
🚨 THE FATAL ERROR

NEVER use the Present Perfect with specific finished time words. They are enemies!

❌ I have developed an app in 2025.
✅ I DEVELOPED an app in 2025. (Specific time = Past Simple)

Reading Context: An Interview with Teacher Ivan

Interviewer: Ivan, you are a very active person! Have you taught at Colegio Fielding for a long time?

Teacher Ivan: Yes, I have taught English and Philosophy here for a few years. I started my journey in 2021. It was a great decision.

Interviewer: I heard you are also a programmer. Is that true?

Teacher Ivan: Yes! I have developed an app for the school called 'Teacher Ivan - Lifestyle'. Actually, I wrote the final HTML code yesterday morning.

Interviewer: Amazing! And what about your free time?

Teacher Ivan: I have competed in many CrossFit events with my girlfriend. We won a medal two months ago. And of course, we spend time with Perlita. We adopted her last year!
