2. Grammar: The Power Words

We use Just, Already, and Yet with the Present Perfect to give extra information about when something happened or if we are still waiting for it. Position is everything!

JUST

Acabo de...
An action that happened a very short time ago (1 or 2 minutes ago).
have/has + JUST + V3

"I'm not hungry. I have just eaten an apple."

ALREADY

Ya (afirmación)
An action that happened sooner than expected. Used in (+) sentences.
have/has + ALREADY + V3

"Don't wash the dishes. I have already washed them."

YET

Todavía / Ya (?)
We expect something to happen. Used ONLY in (-) sentences and (?) questions.
... sentence ... + YET

"I haven't cooked dinner yet." (Todavía no)
"Have you fed the cat yet?" (¿Ya...?)

🚨 THE "YET" POSITION TRAP

In Spanish, we usually put "todavía" or "ya" at the beginning of the sentence (e.g., Todavía no he comido). In English, YET always goes at the very end of the sentence!

❌ I haven't yet ordered delivery.
✅ I haven't ordered delivery YET.

Reading Context: Hungry after CrossFit

Girlfriend: Ivan, I'm starving! I have just finished my CrossFit routine. Have you ordered delivery yet?

Teacher Ivan: No, I haven't ordered it yet. I decided to make salads. I have already chopped the tomatoes and the avocado.

Girlfriend: Great! And what about Perlita? Have you fed her yet?

Teacher Ivan: Oh no, I have just remembered! I haven't fed her yet. Look at her... she is staring at me from the sofa!
