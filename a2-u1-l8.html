A Historic Day in Bolivia

Objective: Tell a story in the past using advanced vocabulary and sequencing words.

1. Vocabulary: History & Philosophy

Let's elevate our English! Use these words to talk about important events.

📜 Declaration Declaración Noun
✍️ Sign Firmar Past: Signed
🕊️ Freedom Libertad Noun
🏛️ Republic República Noun
⚔️ Fight Pelear / Luchar Past: Fought
🦸‍♂️ Hero Héroe Plural: Heroes

2. Grammar: Telling a Story

When we tell a story in the past, we cannot just use a list of verbs. We need Sequencing Words to organize the events in chronological order.

First,
(Primero)
Then,
(Luego)
After that,
(Después de eso)
Finally,
(Finalmente)

Reading Context: A Field Trip in Sucre

Read Teacher Ivan's diary entry about his Philosophy and History class.

"Yesterday, I didn't teach English. I taught Philosophy and History at Colegio Fielding. It was a special day, so I took my students on a field trip.

First, we walked to the historic center of Sucre and we visited the Casa de la Libertad. Then, I explained the history of August 6, 1825. I told them how our heroes fought for many years to get our freedom. After that, we saw the exact room where Simon Bolivar and other leaders signed the Declaration of Independence. They created a new Republic!

Finally, we went back to the school. The students were very inspired. Perlita the cat didn't come with us, of course. She is not interested in history; she only cares about food!"
