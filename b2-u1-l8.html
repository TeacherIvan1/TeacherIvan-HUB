Hitting a Plateau

Objective: Master verbs that change meaning depending on Gerunds (-ING) or Infinitives (TO).

1. Vocabulary: Stagnation & Progress

Learn these advanced collocations to describe when progress stops, and how to fix it.

🧱 Hit a plateau Estancarse / Llegar a una meseta
🔨 Break through Superar (un límite)
⚙️ Tweak a routine Hacer pequeños ajustes a una rutina
😩 Burnout Agotamiento mental/físico

2. Grammar: The Chameleon Verbs

In English, there are three "Chameleon Verbs". If you follow them with an -ING verb, they mean one thing. If you follow them with a TO verb, they mean something completely different. Pay close attention!

The Verb: TRY
Try + ING
To experiment to see if something works or solves a problem.
"If you are tired, try drinking more water."
Try + TO
To attempt something very difficult. You make an effort, but might fail.
"I tried to lift 100kg, but it was too heavy."
The Verb: STOP
Stop + ING
To quit an action completely. You don't do it anymore.
"I stopped eating junk food last year."
Stop + TO
To interrupt what you are doing in order to do something else.
"I was driving, and I stopped to answer my phone."
The Verb: REMEMBER
Remember + ING
A memory of the past. You did the action, and now you recall it.
"I remember winning my first medal."
Remember + TO
A task you must not forget to do in the future/present.
"Please, remember to lock the door."

Reading Context: Breaking the Wall

"Recently, my girlfriend and I hit a plateau in our CrossFit training. We weren't getting any stronger, and we started feeling burnout. I tried to lift my personal best, but I couldn't move the bar. It was so frustrating!"

"Our coach gave us some advice. He told us to try tweaking our diet. So, we stopped eating heavy meals before training. Also, during the workouts, we stopped to drink water more often to stay hydrated."

"It worked! We broke through the plateau. I will always remember feeling so happy when I finally lifted that weight. Now, I always remember to stretch before I start."

"Perlita the cat, however, never hits a plateau. She tries to sleep 16 hours a day, and she always succeeds!"
