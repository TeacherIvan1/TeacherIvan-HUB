Pie Charts & Proportions

Objective: Describe fractions, allocate data to categories, and master the "Respectively" rule.

1. Academic Vocabulary: Fractions

To score highly in Lexical Resource, do not repeat the word "percent". Use these fractions instead.

🍕 A quarter Un cuarto (25%) "Exactly a quarter of..."
📐 A third Un tercio (≈33%) "Roughly a third of..."
🌗 Exactly half Exactamente la mitad (50%) "Accounted for half of..."
🧩 The remaining portion La porción restante "The remainder..."

2. The Language of Proportion

When describing a Pie Chart, you need verbs that mean "representar" or "constituir". The best verbs for this are Account for, Make up, and Comprise.

Describing a single slice
Subject + accounted for + [Fraction]

Use this to present the data of one specific category.

"Protein accounted for a third of the total diet."
"Carbohydrates made up exactly half."
The "Respectively" Rule
A and B were X and Y, respectively.

A high-level technique to list two categories and two numbers efficiently in the same sentence.

"Apples and Bananas made up 10% and 5% respectively."
🚨 THE TINY SLICES TRAP

If a pie chart has 8 different categories, DO NOT write a sentence for each one. The examiner will fall asleep and you will lose points. You must group the tiny slices together.

✅ "The remaining 15% was comprised of smaller categories, including vitamins and minerals."

Model Answer Analysis: The Athlete vs. The Feline

Imagine two pie charts. They display the daily macronutrient intake (Carbohydrates, Protein, Fat) of Teacher Ivan during a CrossFit competition week, compared to the diet of his cat, Perlita.

The two pie charts illustrate the daily macronutrient distribution in the diets of a human athlete, Teacher Ivan, and his domestic cat, Perlita.

Overall, there is a striking difference between the two diets. While the human relies heavily on carbohydrates for energy, the feline's nutritional intake is overwhelmingly dominated by fats.

Looking specifically at the athlete’s chart, carbohydrates accounted for exactly half (50%) of his total daily intake, which is essential for demanding sports like CrossFit. Protein also made up a significant proportion, representing roughly a third (30%) of the diet to aid muscle recovery. The remaining portion consisted entirely of fats, which comprised 20%.

In stark contrast, Perlita’s dietary breakdown shows an entirely different pattern. Fats, primarily derived from tuna and resting habits, constituted the vast majority of her intake at an astonishing 70%. Protein and carbohydrates made up 25% and a mere 5% respectively. This demonstrates that while the cat consumed exactly a quarter of her diet as protein, her need for energetic carbohydrates was almost non-existent.
