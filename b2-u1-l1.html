I have been running for years

Objective: Focus on the duration of ongoing actions using the Present Perfect Continuous.

1. Vocabulary: Fitness & Consistency

At the B2 level, we need more advanced verbs and phrases to talk about habits.

🏃‍♂️ Build endurance Desarrollar resistencia
🏋️‍♂️ Lift weights Levantar pesas
💦 Sweat Sudar
📈 Stay consistent Mantenerse constante

2. Grammar: Present Perfect Continuous

We use this tense to emphasize the duration of an action that started in the past and is still happening right now (or has just finished and we can see the physical evidence, like sweat).

Subject + have/has been + Verb(-ing)
FocusExampleContext
Duration (Time)
With For/Since		 I have been teaching English since 2021. I started in 2021, and I am still teaching today.
Physical Evidence
Recent continuous action		 Ivan is very tired and sweating. He has been lifting weights. The action just stopped, but the physical result is obvious.
🚨 The "State Verbs" Trap

At B2 level, you MUST remember this rule: Verbs that describe states (thoughts, feelings, possession) NEVER take the "-ing" form.

Examples of State Verbs: Know, Like, Love, Want, Need, Believe, Belong. Even if the duration continues, you must use the Present Perfect Simple for these verbs!

❌ I have been knowing my best friend for 10 years.
✅ I have KNOWN my best friend for 10 years.

Reading Context: The CrossFit Mindset

Sarah: Teacher Ivan, you have so much energy for your Philosophy classes. What is your secret?

Teacher Ivan: Well, I have been building my endurance for a long time. My girlfriend and I have been doing CrossFit in Sucre for years. It requires a lot of discipline.

Sarah: Wow. And what did you do this morning?

Teacher Ivan: I'm actually exhausted right now. I have been lifting heavy weights since 6:00 AM! I am covered in sweat.

Sarah: And what about Perlita?

Teacher Ivan: Oh, Perlita is the opposite of consistent. She has been sleeping on my laptop all morning. I have known that cat for over a year, and she never exercises!
