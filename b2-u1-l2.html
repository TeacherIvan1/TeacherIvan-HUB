2. Grammar: The Time Machine (Past Perfect)

When you tell a story, you usually establish a "main event" in the Past Simple. But what if you need to explain the background story? What happened before that main event?

You need a Time Machine! The Past Perfect (`had + V3`) and Past Perfect Continuous (`had been + V-ing`) allow you to jump further back in time.

The Narrative Timeline

TenseFormulaUse
Past Perfect Simple had + V3 A completed action that happened before another past action.
(I had packed my bag before we left).
Past Perfect Continuous had been + V-ing Focuses on the duration of an action leading up to a past event.
(I had been training for 6 months).
🚨 The "And Then" Trap

In B1, students tell stories like a robot: "I woke up, and then I ate, and then I went to the gym." In B2, you must sound natural by changing the sequence!

✅ "When I arrived at the gym, the competition had already started."

Reading Context: The Big Competition

"Last weekend, my girlfriend and I went to La Paz for a major CrossFit competition. We felt very confident because we had been training hard for six months. We had stuck to a strict routine every single day in Sucre.

However, when we arrived at our hotel, I realized there was a problem. I opened my gym bag and saw my lifting belt. It was ruined! Perlita the cat had scratched it the night before while I was sleeping!

Despite that obstacle, we did a great job. By the time the event finished, we had pushed our limits completely."
