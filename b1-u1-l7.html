2. Grammar: Modals of Advice & Obligation

When someone is stressed, we give them advice or tell them the rules they need to follow. We use Modal Verbs before the main verb.

SHOULD

Good Idea
(Consejo)
Use this to give friendly advice. It is a recommendation, not a rule.
"You should take a break. You look tired."

MUST

Strong Advice / Internal Rule
Use this when you strongly believe it is necessary, or for personal rules.
"I am very stressed. I must sleep more tonight."

HAVE TO

External Obligation
Use this for rules made by other people (school, work, doctors).
"Students have to pass the English exam."
🚨 THE "TO" TRAP

Modal verbs are strong! They NEVER use the word "to" before the main action verb. (Exception: Have to, because "to" is part of its name).

❌ You should to meditate.
✅ You should meditate.
✅ You must breathe.

Reading Context: Exam Season at Colegio Fielding

It is exam season at Colegio Fielding. The students are very nervous. As a Philosophy teacher, I always give them this advice:

"You have to study hard, but you mustn't panic. If you feel anxious, you should breathe deeply and take a break. You must remember that mental health is more important than a grade. You shouldn't overthink every question."

"To relieve my own stress, I have to go to my CrossFit classes. Or sometimes, I just sit on the sofa and pet Perlita. You should try petting a cat; it's very relaxing!"
