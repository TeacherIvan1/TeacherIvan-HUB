2. Grammar: The Magic Eraser

To make a negative sentence in the past, we use the auxiliary Did not (or the short form Didn't) for ALL pronouns.

Subject + DIDN'T + Base Verb (Normal)
🪄 THE MAGIC ERASER TRAP!

At Colegio Fielding, this is the #1 mistake in A2! Think of Didn't as a magic eraser. When you write Didn't, it erases the past tense of the main verb.

I didn't walked. ➔ I didn't WALK.
She didn't went. ➔ She didn't GO.
We didn't stayed. ➔ We didn't STAY.

Reading Context: A Lazy Sunday in Sucre

"Last Sunday was a very rainy day in Sucre. My girlfriend and I didn't wake up early. We didn't go to the CrossFit gym because of the weather. But we didn't stay at home all day, either! We went to the city center, but we didn't walk; we took a taxi. We ate some delicious salteñas, so we didn't cook dinner. And what about Perlita? She didn't catch any mice. She just slept on the sofa!"
