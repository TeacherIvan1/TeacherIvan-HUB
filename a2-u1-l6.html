2. Grammar: Questions with "Did"

To ask a question in the Past Simple (except for the verb To Be), we ALWAYS start with the auxiliary word Did. Just like "Didn't", the word "Did" acts as a magic eraser!

DID + Subject + Base Verb (Normal) + ?

In English, we don't just say "Yes" or "No". We use Short Answers to sound polite and natural.

QuestionShort Answer (+)Short Answer (-)
Did you travel to La Paz? Yes, I did. No, I didn't.
Did she rent a car? Yes, she did. No, she didn't.
Did they like the museum? Yes, they did. No, they didn't.
🚨 THE DOUBLE PAST TRAP

Remember the magic eraser rule! If you use "Did" at the beginning, the main verb CANNOT be in the past tense. You only need ONE sign of the past in the sentence.

Did you went to the beach? ➔ Did you GO to the beach?
Did he stayed in a hotel? ➔ Did he STAY in a hotel?
