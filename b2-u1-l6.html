2. Grammar: The Process vs The Result

How do you choose between the two Present Perfects? It depends on what you want to emphasize! Are you focusing on the sweat and time (Process) or the numbers and completion (Result)?

The PROCESS (Continuous)
have/has been + ING
Focuses on the action itself and its visible side effects (like exhaustion, sweat, or dirt).
"I am out of breath because I have been running."
The RESULT (Simple)
have/has + V3
Focuses on the completion of the action. Often answers "How much?" or "How many?".
"I have run 10 kilometers today."
🚨 The "How Much" vs "How Long" Rule

If a sentence mentions a quantity (3 cups of coffee, 50 burpees, 2 books), you CANNOT use the continuous form.

❌ I have been reading three books today. (Incorrect!)
✅ I have READ three books today. (Result = Simple)
✅ I have BEEN READING all day. (Duration = Continuous)

Reading Context: Post-Workout Reality

"It is March 2026, and the CrossFit Open is officially here in Sucre! I just got home, and I need a minute to catch my breath. I have been competing in the arena all morning, and my muscles are incredibly sore. I am completely burned out!"

"But the exhaustion is worth it. So far today, I have accomplished two new personal records, and I have finished in the top 10 of my category!"

"When I opened the door, the living room was a disaster. Perlita has been playing with the sofa cushions again. She has completely destroyed one of them! While I have won a medal, she has won a one-way ticket to the garden!"
