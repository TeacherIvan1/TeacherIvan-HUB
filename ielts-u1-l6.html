Describing a Process

Objective: Outline manufacturing or development stages using Sequencing Connectors and the Passive Voice.

1. Academic Vocabulary: Sequencing

To move from one step of the flowchart to the next, abandon basic words like "Next" or "Then". Use these instead:

1️⃣ Initially Inicialmente / Para empezar Synonym: In the first stage
➡️ Subsequently Posteriormente Synonym: Following this
🔄 Once [A] is completed Una vez que [A] está terminado Use: To link two stages smoothly
🏁 Ultimately Por último / Finalmente Synonym: In the final stage

2. The "Factory" Voice (Present Simple Passive)

When you describe a process, the examiner does not care who is doing the work. The focus must be entirely on the product. You must use the Passive Voice to achieve a formal, objective tone.

The Active Mistake
Worker + Verb + Object

This sounds informal and conversational. Avoid it in Process tasks.

"A programmer writes the HTML code."
The IELTS Passive Standard
Object + is/are + Past Participle (V3)

This sounds academic, objective, and professional.

"The HTML code is written."

The Overview Rule for Processes:

For your Overview paragraph, you must count the total number of stages, identify the very first step, and identify the final outcome.

🚨 THE FORGOTTEN "TO BE"

Because Spanish speakers often translate directly, they sometimes forget the verb "is" or "are" in passive sentences. This destroys the grammar score!

❌ Subsequently, the application tested for bugs. (The app tested itself?)
✅ Subsequently, the application IS tested for bugs.
