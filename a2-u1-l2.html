Was it a good holiday?

Objective: Ask questions and give short answers about past events using Was/Were.

1. Vocabulary: Describing a Trip

Learn these adjectives to describe how your holidays or vacations were.

☀️ Sunny Soleado
👥 Crowded Lleno de gente
🍽️ Delicious Delicioso (Comida)
😫 Exhausting Agotador
😌 Relaxing Relajante

2. Key Grammar: Questions & Short Answers

To make a question in the past, move Was or Were to the beginning of the sentence. Do not answer just "Yes" or "No". In English, we use Short Answers to be polite.

Question (?)Short Answer (+)Short Answer (-)
Was the hotel good? Yes, it was. No, it wasn't.
Were you happy? Yes, I was. No, I wasn't.
Were the museums open? Yes, they were. No, they weren't.
🚨 THE "DID" TRAP

A very common mistake is using "Did" with the verb To Be. Remember: Was/Were are strong verbs, they NEVER need the auxiliary "Did" to make a question.

Did you be at the beach? ➔ WERE you at the beach?
Did the food be delicious? ➔ WAS the food delicious?

Let's see it in context: Back from Vacation

Sarah: Welcome back to Colegio Fielding, Teacher Ivan! Was it a good holiday?

Teacher Ivan: Yes, it was! My girlfriend and I travelled for a CrossFit competition. The trip was exhausting, but the competition was amazing.

Sarah: Were the restaurants good?

Teacher Ivan: Yes, they were. The food was delicious, but the places were very crowded.

Sarah: And was Perlita the cat with you?

Teacher Ivan: No, she wasn't! She stayed here in Sucre. She hates traveling!
