Paraphrasing the Prompt

Objective: Rewrite the IELTS prompt using synonyms and structural changes to create a Band 8.0 Introduction.

1. Academic Vocabulary: Synonym Surgery

The first sentence of your essay must be a paraphrase of the prompt. Memorize these direct replacements to save time.

📊 Shows Muestra Use: Illustrates / Demonstrates
ℹ️ Information Información Use: Data / Figures
👥 People Personas Use: Individuals / Demographics
📅 From X to Y De X a Y (Tiempo) Use: Between X and Y

2. The 3-Step Paraphrasing Engine

To avoid the penalty for copying, you must change the prompt. A Band 5.0 student only changes one word. A Band 8.0 student uses the 3-Step Engine: 1. Synonyms, 2. Word Class, 3. Order.

1. Synonym Replacement
Replace basic nouns and verbs with academic equivalents.
The graph shows...
The provided line chart illustrates...
2. Changing Word Class / Voice
Change a verb into a noun, or change Active Voice to Passive Voice.
...how students use the app...
...the usage of the application by learners...
🚨 THE COPY-PASTE PENALTY

If the prompt is exactly 20 words, and you copy it perfectly into your introduction, the examiner will subtract 20 words from your final word count. If you fall below the 150-word minimum, you lose severe points. Paraphrasing is mandatory!

Model Answer Analysis: The CrossFit Popularity Prompt

Let's look at an original IELTS prompt given to students at Colegio Fielding, and how we transform it into a Band 8.0 Introduction.

The Original Exam Prompt:

"The graph below shows how many hours students at Colegio Fielding spent on the Teacher Ivan app and doing CrossFit from January to March 2026."

The Band 8.0 Paraphrased Introduction:

"The provided line chart illustrates the amount of time that secondary learners allocated to digital application usage and physical training over a three-month period in 2026."

Why is this a Band 8.0?

  • "shows" became ➔ illustrates
  • "how many hours" became ➔ the amount of time (Changing word class)
  • "students" became ➔ secondary learners
  • "Teacher Ivan app and CrossFit" became ➔ digital application usage and physical training (Generalization)
  • "from January to March" became ➔ over a three-month period (Rephrasing time)

Of course, if Perlita were in the prompt, "sleeping on the sofa" would become "feline resting periods". Always elevate the vocabulary!
