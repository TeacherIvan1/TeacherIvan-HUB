Have you ever tried CrossFit?

Objective: Talk about life experiences using Present Perfect and "Ever/Never".

1. Vocabulary: Extreme Experiences

To talk about experiences, we must use the 3rd column of verbs: The Past Participle (V3).

🏋️‍♂️ Try CrossFit Probar CrossFit V3: Tried
🏆 Win a competition Ganar una competencia V3: Won
🦴 Break a bone Romperse un hueso V3: Broken
🍣 Eat exotic food Comer comida exótica V3: Eaten

2. Grammar: Present Perfect (Experiences)

We use the Present Perfect to talk about experiences in our life. It doesn't matter when it happened, only that it happened in your lifetime.

Subject + HAVE / HAS + Past Participle (V3)
TypeStructure & KeywordsExample
Questions
(¿Alguna vez...?)		 Have/Has + subject + EVER + V3 ? Have you ever tried CrossFit?
Positive
(Sí)		 Yes, I have / Yes, she has. Yes, I have. I have won a medal.
Negative
(Nunca)		 Subject + Have/Has + NEVER + V3 No, I have never broken a bone.
🚨 THE "NEVER" RULE

The word Never is already negative. Therefore, you CANNOT use "haven't" with "never". It's a double negative!

❌ I haven't never eaten mondongo.
✅ I have never eaten mondongo.

Reading Context: Chatting at the Gym

Sarah: Teacher Ivan, you look very tired today! Have you ever tried resting?

Teacher Ivan: Haha, yes, I have. But my girlfriend and I are training hard. We have won a few competitions in La Paz recently.

Sarah: Wow! Have you ever broken a bone doing CrossFit?

Teacher Ivan: No, luckily I have never broken anything. We are very careful.

Sarah: And what about Perlita? Has she ever caught a mouse?

Teacher Ivan: No, she hasn't! She has never done any exercise in her life!
