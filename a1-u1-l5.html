2. Key Grammar: Expressing Age

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE ALERT

In Spanish, we "have" years (Tengo 20 años). In English, we DO NOT use the verb "to have". In English, we use the verb "To Be" (I am, You are, He is...).

I have 20 years. ➔ I am 20 years old.

To ask about age, we say:
"How old are you?" (¿Qué edad tienes?)

Let's see it in context:

Teacher Ivan: Welcome to the class. How old are you?

Student: I am fifteen years old. And you, teacher?

Teacher Ivan: I am thirty years old.

Student: And how old is my classmate, Sarah?

Teacher Ivan: She is sixteen.
