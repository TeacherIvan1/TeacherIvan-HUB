Bar Charts & Comparisons

Objective: Group data effectively and use advanced contrast language to describe Bar Charts.

1. Academic Vocabulary: Proportions

Instead of just listing numbers (e.g., 80%, 5%), use these phrases to show proportions.

👥 Outnumber Superar en número a... Formula: A outnumbered B
🍰 A mere fraction Una pequeña fracción Meaning: Very small amount
📊 The vast majority La gran mayoría Meaning: Over 75%
⚖️ A striking difference Una diferencia notable Synonym: A clear contrast

2. The Grouping Strategy & Connectors

A Bar Chart usually has categories (e.g., Countries, Activities, Ages). The biggest mistake is describing Bar 1, then Bar 2, then Bar 3. You MUST group them! Group the highest numbers in one paragraph, and the lowest in another, linking them with contrast words.

While / Whereas
While [Sentence A], [Sentence B].

Used to contrast two ideas in the same sentence.

"Whereas reading was popular, sports were ignored."
In stark contrast,
[Sentence A]. In stark contrast, [Sentence B].

Used at the beginning of a new sentence to introduce an opposite idea.

"Gaming was at 80%. In stark contrast, studying was at 5%."
🚨 THE FRAGMENT TRAP

Words like While and Whereas are conjunctions. They CANNOT stand alone as a sentence. If you put a period before the second idea, you will lose points for Grammar.

❌ While Ivan likes CrossFit. Perlita likes sleeping. (Grammar Fragment!)
✅ While Ivan likes CrossFit, Perlita likes sleeping.

Model Answer Analysis: Weekend Habits

Imagine a Bar Chart comparing the preferred weekend activities of secondary students at Colegio Fielding in Sucre (March 2026). The categories are: Sleeping, Using Lifestyle Apps, CrossFit/Sports, and Studying Philosophy.

The provided bar chart compares the percentage of secondary students at Colegio Fielding who engaged in four different weekend activities in March 2026.

Overall, it is immediately apparent that passive activities were significantly more favored by the students. While resting and digital entertainment accounted for the highest figures, academic and physical pursuits were chosen by a mere fraction of the demographic.

Looking at the most popular choices, sleeping was the dominant weekend activity, chosen by the vast majority (85%) of the students, perhaps adopting habits similar to local pets like Perlita the cat. Using mobile apps, such as the newly released 'Teacher Ivan - Lifestyle', followed closely behind, capturing 70% of the students' preference.

In stark contrast, active and academic hobbies recorded strikingly low numbers. Engaging in sports like CrossFit appealed to only 15% of the secondary students. Furthermore, studying Philosophy was the least popular activity overall at a minimal 5%. Consequently, the preference for sleeping outnumbered the preference for studying Philosophy by a ratio of seventeen to one.
